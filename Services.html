Our Services

Early Childhood Intervention

Here the speech therapist promotes speech and language development during the “critical period” of development.

Articulation disorders

Difficulty pronouncing words correctly which affects listeners’ ability to understand what’s being said.

Language disorders (receptive and expressive language)- 

Difficulty forming words and/or sentences. Development occurs out of sequence- speech and language sounds unusual when talking to others and finds it difficult to understand language when being spoken to.

Language delays (receptive and expressive language) 

Development occurs within sequence but is slower than that of a typically developing child- sounds like a younger child when talking and takes longer to understand language when being spoken to.

Cognitive- communication disorders

Difficulties that arise in areas affecting communication skills involving memory, orientation, logic and reasoning, problem solving and perception.

Autism

Delayed and/or disordered speech and language development, poor social skills, and behaviour issues.

Fluency disorders

Is often referred to as stuttering. Can be seen when a child repeats sounds or syllables when speaking, hesitates when speaking, has blocks and/or extra bodily movements.

Dysphagia (feeding and swallowing disorders)

Difficulty experienced when eating/drinking. This includes difficulty with chewing, swallowing, coughing and food refusal.
